The Mare of Night's Eternal Tide

~ A Pirate's Decree Regarding the Scrolls of Your Data ~

✦ ✦ ✦

I. The Legend of the Mare in the Dark Waters

Harken, ye brave souls who sail the ink-black seas beneath the twilight sky! It be said among the ancient corsairs that Mare of the Night rides the deep swells when the last lantern fades and the moon hangs heavy like a doubloon cast into a bottomless trench. She gallops across the churning foam, her mane a cascade of midnight stars, her hooves striking sparks upon the crests of waves that no mortal ship dare cross alone. Her legend is as old as the first sailor who ever dared look up and reckon by the stars.

II. When Twilight Spreads Her Crimson Sails

In the hour between the dying sun and the rising dark, when the horizon bleeds in shades of violet and amber — that be Twilight's hour. The corsairs of the Celestial Fleet lower their voices then, for it is whispered that the Mare speaks through the fading light itself. Her words come not in thunder, but in the hush of a wave retreating from the shore, pulling secrets and forgotten names back into the deep. Many a sailor has glimpsed her silhouette against that bruised and burning sky, standing proud upon a crest, eyes like twin silver moons, watching — always watching — as the world surrenders its colours to the night.

III. The Oath of the Nocturnal Brotherhood

The Corsairs who sail under the Banner of the Crescent Moon swear an oath upon their first twilight at sea: that no treasure plundered from the depths shall be hoarded beyond its worth, and no name written in the ship's log shall remain there longer than the voyage demands. What is taken may be returned. What is recorded may be struck from the ledger. This is the Mare's own law — older than any king's decree — and the pirates of the Night Tide honour it above all edicts of the waking world.

⚔ The Rite of Data Deletion ⚔

Should ye wish yer name stricken from the ship's manifest and yer particulars cast into the sea, send word to the Harbourmaster by post-bird or carrier wave. Dispatch yer petition to delete@yourdomain.com and include yer pirate alias (username) and the vessel (platform) upon which ye sailed. Within 30 tides — no more — yer records shall be surrendered to the deep, never to resurface in these waters again. The Mare of Night accepts no half-measures.

IV. The Night Knows What the Day Forgets

As the twilight gives way fully to darkness and the Mare gallops free across the heavens, remember this, ye who trusted us with yer passage: the night is not a place of loss, but of honourable returning. Every secret kept has a tide that brings it home. Every scroll sealed has a flame that can unseal it. We hold yer data as a ship holds its crew — with care, with purpose, and with the solemn promise that when the voyage ends and ye call for the gangplank, ye shall leave as freely as ye came aboard, with nothing held behind in the hold save the memory of fair winds shared.

⛵ May yer sails be full and yer nights be clear.
